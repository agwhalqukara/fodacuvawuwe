Early Life and Biography

Julian Lennon was born on 8 April 1963 at Sefton General Hospital in Liverpool, United Kingdom, to John Lennon and Cynthia (John Lennon’s first wife). He was named after his parental grandmother, who died before his birth. The Beatles manager, Brian Epstein, became his godfather. He went to an independent boarding school, Ruthin School. His parents got divorced when he was only five due to his father’s infidelity with Yoko Ona, a Japanese Multimedia artist, through which he has a half-brother, Sean Lennon.

The divorce of his parents led to a mark on him and stopped visiting his father. But later, on request of his father’s then-girlfriend May Pang, he started to meet his father. John Lennon brought him a guitar and drum machine for Christmas, which encouraged him to develop an interest in music.

Personal Life

In commemoration of his father’s 70th birthday, he and his mother, unveiled the John Lennon Peace Monument in his home town of Liverpool, on 9 October 2010.

Career

Lennon made his musical debut at a young age of 11 on his father’s album “walls and bridges,” playing drums on “ya-ya.” In the 1980s, according to All Music, he had a remarkable similarity to his father’s vocal similarity.

His debut album “Valotte” was a huge success which was released in 1984, it spawned two top 10 hits, and the title track and “too late for goodbyes” earned him a nomination for Grammy Award for Best New Artist in 1985.

Lennon left the music business for years in 1990 to follow his interest in cooking, sailing, and sculpting. By 1998 he released the album “Photograph Smile” too little commercial success, which received a lot of praise.

In 2006 he ventured into internet business, including Mystore.com with Todd Meagher and Bebo founder Michael Birch.

Photography

After photographing his half-brother Sean’s musical tour in 2007, he took a serious interest in photography.

On 17 Sept 2010, Lennon opened an exhibition of 35 photographs called “Timeless: The Photography of Julian Lennon” with the help of Timothy (his friend and fellow photographer). The photograph includes shots of Sean and U2 frontman Bono.
